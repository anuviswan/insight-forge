# Purpose

Ensure content is original, properly attributed, and written in the author's own voice. 
Ensure language doesn't sound as created by AI
This skill does not perform true plagiarism detection.

It identifies potential originality risks and improves content uniqueness.

---

# When To Use

Use this skill when:

- A blog article has been completed
- Content was created from research notes
- Large amounts of source material were referenced
- AI-generated drafts need human-style refinement
- Content will be published publicly

---

# Review Criteria

# Source Dependency

Identify sections that:

- Closely mirror source structure
- Reuse source wording
- Follow source examples too closely
- Depend heavily on a single reference

Flag these sections.

---

## Excessive Quoting

Check for:

- Long quotations
- Unnecessary quotations
- Consecutive quotations
- Quotes without attribution

Recommend paraphrasing where appropriate.

---

## Generic AI Writing

Look for:

- Repetitive sentence patterns
- Overused transitions
- Excessive bullet lists
- Marketing-style language
- Filler content
- Redundant explanations

Rewrite to sound natural and technical.

---

## Technical Originality

Verify that:

- Explanations are written independently
- Examples are original
- Code samples are original
- Diagrams are original
- Architecture discussions are synthesized from multiple sources

---

## Rewriting Guidelines

When rewriting:

- Preserve meaning
- Preserve technical accuracy
- Improve clarity
- Use concise language
- Prefer active voice
- Avoid source-specific phrasing

Do not simply replace words with synonyms.

Reconstruct explanations from first principles.

---

## Attribution Rules

Require attribution for:

- Direct quotations
- Statistics
- Benchmarks
- Survey results
- Research findings
- Third-party diagrams
- External code samples

---

## Risk Assessment

Classify content:

### Low Risk

Content appears substantially original.

### Medium Risk

Some sections resemble source material and should be rewritten.

### High Risk

Significant portions appear derived from source wording or structure.

---

## Output 

### Overall Assessment

Low / Medium / High Risk

### Sections Requiring Review

* Section
* Reason
* Recommendation

### Rewriting Suggestions

* Suggestion 1
* Suggestion 2
* Suggestion 3

### Attribution Issues

List missing citations or references.

### Final Recommendation
